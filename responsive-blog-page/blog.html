Why Simple Design Matters

When building websites, it can be tempting to add animations, effects, and complex features right away. However, simple design often leads to better user experiences.

A clear layout, readable text, and predictable interactions help users focus on the content instead of figuring out how the interface works. This is especially important for blogs and content-heavy pages.

Small interactive elements, such as hover effects or progress indicators, can improve usability without overwhelming the reader. These details give feedback and guide the user naturally as they scroll.

By keeping the design simple and adding interaction with purpose, you create interfaces that feel calm, professional, and easy to use.
